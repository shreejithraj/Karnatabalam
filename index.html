About Karnata Balam

Our Mission

Karnata Balam (Kechhedeya Kannadigara Sene) is dedicated to fighting corruption and delivering free legal awareness from qualified professionals to the underprivileged. We stand as guardians of social equality, empowerment, and cultural preservation.

Our Vision

To create a society where every Kannadiga is empowered with knowledge of their rights, where corruption finds no place, and where our rich cultural heritage continues to inspire future generations. We believe in the strength of unity and the power of awareness.

Immudi Pulakeshi - The Great Chalukya Emperor

Pulakeshi II (Immudi Pulakeshi) was one of the greatest rulers of the Chalukya dynasty who reigned from 610 to 642 CE. Known for his military prowess and administrative excellence, he expanded the Chalukya empire across the Deccan plateau, making it one of the most powerful kingdoms of ancient India. His reign marked the golden age of Chalukya architecture and culture.

Under his patronage, magnificent temples and cave complexes were constructed, including the famous rock-cut temples at Badami, Aihole, and Pattadakal. These architectural marvels stand as testimony to the advanced engineering skills and artistic sensibilities of the Chalukya period. Pulakeshi II's military campaigns extended the empire's influence from the Arabian Sea to the Bay of Bengal, establishing Karnataka as a major power center in medieval India.
